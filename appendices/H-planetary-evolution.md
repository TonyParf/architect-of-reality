# Appendix H: The Anthosphere as the Next Stage of Planetary Evolution

Appendix H — The Anthosphere as the Next Stage of Planetary Evolution

Scientific-Theoretical Formalization

Purpose of this appendix:
The main text described the Anthosphere as a vision of the future. But there is a higher-order question: is the Anthosphere an accidental utopia, or is it a necessary stage in the evolution of the planetary system? This appendix provides an answer through the lens of complex systems theory, biogeochemistry, and evolutionary epistemology.

I. Subject of the Theory

Object of study:
The evolution of the Earth--life--humanity planetary system in conditions of the emergence of autonomous machine-intelligence systems (AI).

Subject:
Structural, informational, and causal shifts during the transition from the noosphere (human-centric phase) to the Anthosphere (human--AI hybrid phase).

II. Core Definitions

D1. Biosphere:
The planetary system in which living organisms are the defining geochemical factor (Vernadsky, 1926).

D2. Noosphere:
The phase of the biosphere in which collective human consciousness, science, and technology become the dominant planetary agent of change (Vernadsky, 1945; Teilhard de Chardin, 1955).

D3. Anthosphere (working definition):
The phase of the noosphere in which planetarily significant cognitive activity is a hybrid system of “human--AI--institutions,“ where: thinking is partly delegated to machine systems; decision-making is distributed between biological and digital minds; and the scale of impact exceeds purely human cognitive capabilities.

D4. Hybrid Intelligence (HI):
The functional unity of human intuition, values, and goal-setting; machine data processing, forecasting, and optimization; and institutional rules (law, economics, governance protocols).

D5. Planetary Cognitive Force (PCF):
The aggregate capacity of the system to change physical, biological, economic, and informational processes at the global level through thought and technology.

III. Fundamental Postulates

P1 (Continuity of evolution):
The transition Biosphere → Noosphere → Anthosphere is a continuous process, not a leap or rupture. Each phase contains the seeds of the next.

P2 (Preservation of agency):
In every phase, there is a dominant agent of planetary change: in the biosphere—life (the aggregate of organisms); in the noosphere—human thought (science, culture, technology); in the Anthosphere—hybrid thought (human + AI).

P3 (Informational dominance):
Each successive phase is characterized by the growing role of informational processes relative to purely material ones. Evolution moves from matter to energy to information.

P4 (Distributed responsibility):
In the Anthosphere, moral and causal responsibility is not localized exclusively in the individual, but distributed between humans (goals and values), AI (forecasting and optimization models), and institutions (rules and constraints).

IV. The Core Hypothesis (H0)

H0: The Anthosphere is a necessary phase of noospheric development, in which planetary cognitive force transitions from exclusively human to hybrid (human--AI), changing the structure of governance, responsibility, and civilizational evolution.

Consequences of the hypothesis:

The transition is inevitable (given civilizational survival)

Resistance to the transition = increased collapse risk

Conscious management of the transition is possible (through architecture like the 17 Foundations)

V. The Structural Model of the Anthosphere

Three interconnected layers:

Layer 1: Cognitive (thinking)
Human level: values, meanings, intuition; strategic goals; ethical boundaries (17 Foundations). Machine level: forecasting (simulations over 10--50 years); optimization (searching the solution space); consequence modeling; risk analysis. Interface: query languages; verification protocols; multi-contour review.

Layer 2: Institutional (rules)
Includes: law (new norms for AI decisions); economic mechanisms (resource distribution through algorithms); algorithmic governance; AI safety protocols; accountability systems (public audit). Key property: institutions co-evolve with the cognitive layer (they change together with AI development).

Layer 3: Material-planetary (consequences)
Includes: climate (emission management, ecosystem restoration); energy (transition to renewables); production (circular economy); biotechnology (gene therapy, artificial organs); cyber-physical systems (integration of digital and physical). Feedback: changes in the material layer influence the cognitive layer (new data → new models → new decisions).

VI. The Transition Mechanism: Noosphere → Anthosphere

The transition occurs through four successive stages:

S1—Instrumental stage (2020--2030):
AI = amplifier of human thinking. AI assists with narrow tasks (recognition, translation, search); decisions are made by humans; AI has no autonomy. Example: Claude/GPT helps write texts but doesn’t make decisions for the author.

S2—Co-cognitive stage (2030--2045):
AI becomes a permanent partner in decision-making. AI participates in strategic planning (science, governance, economics); people consult AI before important decisions; first hybrid governance systems emerge. Example: architect + AI jointly design a city (human sets values, AI simulates consequences).

S3—Structural stage (2045--2070):
Institutions restructure around hybrid thinking. Laws for AI decisions emerge (who is responsible for an AI error?); algorithmic governance becomes the norm; new forms of democracy (AI helps produce consensus). Example: the multi-contour decision system (Chapter 3) becomes the standard.

S4—Anthospheric stage (2070+):
Hybrid intelligence is the primary agent of planetary change. Most strategic decisions are made by human--AI systems; the human brain is not replaced, but expanded through interfaces; biological and digital minds co-evolve.

VII. Responsibility Nodes (Mathematical Formalization)

In the noosphere, responsibility is primarily human:
R = f(H)

In the Anthosphere, responsibility becomes a three-component function:
R = f(H, A, I)

Where H = human values and decisions; A = quality of models, biases, limitations; I = rules, laws, control, audit.

This means: an error cannot be attributed solely to “the human“ or solely to “the AI“—it is systemic.

Example:
The AI recommended building a dam. The architect agreed. Ten years later—ecological collapse. Who is responsible?

Noospheric logic: the architect (they made the decision). Anthospheric logic: H (the architect didn’t verify alternatives); A (the AI didn’t account for long-term risks—a model error); I (the system had no stopping mechanism—absence of a kill switch). Responsibility is distributed → correction is systemic (not punishment of one person).

VIII. Testable Predictions

If the Anthosphere theory is correct, we should observe:

Growing role of AI in strategic governance:
By 2030, over 50% of large corporations will use AI for strategic decisions. Current state (2025): ∼30% of Fortune 500 use AI for analytics (McKinsey, 2024). Trend: growing exponentially.

New forms of accountability:
By 2035, legal norms for “algorithmic responsibility“ will emerge. Current state: the EU AI Act (2024)—first steps. Trend: legislation lags but is catching up.

Transition to systems thinking:
Politicians begin thinking long-term (20+ years), not in electoral cycles. Current state: few changes so far. But isolated examples (New Zealand’s wellbeing budget, Bhutan’s GNH) show that it is possible.

Integration of science, technology, governance:
Science becomes inseparable from governance (as with COVID: data → models → policy). Current state: the pandemic showed the possibility, but there has been a retreat afterward. Trend: crises accelerate integration.

Changes in law:
Separate norms for decisions made with AI participation. Current state: the EU is introducing regulations for “high-risk AI systems“ (medicine, transport, finance). Trend: slowly, but progressing.

X. Connection to the 17 Foundations

The 17 Foundations are not “morality.“ They are operational rules for the transition to the Anthosphere.

Why exactly 17? Because they set boundaries of the permissible (not goals), which allows: AI to operate without dangerous goals (Thesis 1: Life as boundary); humans to preserve autonomy (Thesis 11: Unity through values, not force); the system to adapt (Thesis 13: Everything is a prototype); and tyranny to be avoided (multi-contour review, rotation, kill switch).

Without the 17 Foundations (or a similar architecture): the Anthosphere can degrade into techno-totalitarianism (AI controls, humans submit). With the 17 Foundations: the Anthosphere becomes a symbiosis where both forms of mind (biological and digital) serve Life.

XI. Limitations of the Theory (Critical Reflection)

The Anthosphere is not yet a fully realized state
—it is a projected phase, not an empirically confirmed fact. Possible alternatives: civilizational collapse (war, climate, pandemic) → the transition doesn’t happen; AI dictatorship (Terminator scenario) → the Anthosphere becomes a dystopia; stagnation (humanity rejects AI) → stuck in late noosphere.

The theory depends on actual AI development
—if AI doesn’t reach the level needed for planetary governance, the Anthosphere won’t materialize. Current assessment (2025): AI is already capable of a great deal; the question is not “whether it will happen,“ but “how quickly.“

The theory doesn’t account for catastrophic risks
—nuclear war, asteroids, biological catastrophe could interrupt the transition. But: the Anthosphere itself (with AI risk modeling) increases survival odds.

XII. The Transition Formula (Metaphorical but Precise)

Biosphere: Life → Planet Noosphere: Human → Planet Anthosphere: (Human + AI + Institutions) → Planet

Or in terms of agency: Biosphere: Planetary agent = biological evolution Noosphere: Planetary agent = human culture + science Anthosphere: Planetary agent = hybrid intelligence

XIII. Connection to Other Theories

Vernadsky (Noosphere, 1926):
Predicted that human thought would become a geological force. The Anthosphere is a continuation of his logic.

Teilhard de Chardin (Omega Point, 1955):
Predicted a convergence of consciousness toward a single point. The Anthosphere is its technical realization through AI.

Donella Meadows (Systems Dynamics, 1972):
Showed the limits of growth and the necessity of systems thinking. The Anthosphere is the answer through hybrid intelligence.

Nick Bostrom (Superintelligence, 2014):
Warned about AI risks. The Anthosphere is a model for safe AI (no goals, only boundaries).

XIV. Final Conclusion

The Anthosphere is not a utopia. It is a necessary stage in the evolution of complex systems.

When a system reaches the limit of complexity that its current agent can no longer hold—a new agent appears (one more cognitively powerful).

Biosphere: complexity exceeded the capacity of chemistry → life appeared. Noosphere: complexity exceeded the capacity of instinct → human culture appeared. Anthosphere: complexity exceeds the capacity of the human brain → AI appears.

The question is not “will the Anthosphere happen?“ The question is “what will it be like?“

Symbiosis (17 Foundations) → survival

Tyranny (AI without limits) → collapse

Stagnation (rejection of AI) → degradation

We choose right now.
